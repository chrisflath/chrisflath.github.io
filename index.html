Prof. Dr. Christoph M. Flath

Chair of Business Information Systems and Business Analytics
Julius-Maximilians-Universität Würzburg

I leverage large-scale behavioral data to understand how humans collaborate, compete, and make decisions. My current work uses competitive gaming as a rich empirical laboratory—applying process mining and machine learning to study team dynamics, skill development, and matchmaking at scale. I also work on operational challenges in retail and energy systems, combining optimization with behavioral insights.

Research Interests

Game Analytics & Esports

Using competitive gaming as an empirical laboratory to study team collaboration, skill development, and matchmaking algorithms at unprecedented scale.

Process Mining & Behavioral Analytics

Extracting insights from event logs to understand how experts and novices differ, how teams form effective strategies, and how complex processes unfold.

Retail Operations & Consumer Behavior

Investigating how operational decisions like delivery speed affect returns, and optimizing inventory routing with machine learning.

Energy Systems & Market Design

Designing tariff structures and coordination mechanisms for electricity markets, with a focus on data-driven customer segmentation.
